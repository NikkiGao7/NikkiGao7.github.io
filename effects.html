Mental health importance & effects

Why is mental health important?

Mental health is an extremely important health aspect especially in youth and teens, because it affects growth and development in adolescence and it affects behaviors and habits in teens and young adults. Having mental challenges can affect many parts of your life and lead to struggles that you might not even realize is caused by mental health. Treating your mental health is just as important as your physical health and if unaddressed as a teen, these issues can be passed into your adulthood.

Youth

Adolescence is a time for developing and growing in many ways. In our youth, we develop patterns, habits, and skills that we carry on for the rest of our lives. Having mental health problems/disorders can interrupt growth and cause many problems with decision making, physical health, and school. According to World Health Organization, “An estimated 10-20% of adolescents globally experience mental health conditions, yet these remain underdiagnosed and undertreated”. Health habits are established in youth as well, and having a mental health problem increases risk in drug use, experiencing violence, and sexual behaviors that can lead to STDs or an unintended pregnancies. These habits will continue into adult years, which is why it is very important for adolescents to pay attention to mental health.

Teens

It is common in teenage years to experience a mental health challenge and if untreated, will affect development and adulthood. 50% of mental health problems are established by age 14. Teens gain social skills, a sense of identity, and independence as they develop, but this development can become more complicated with a mental health issue. According to the Mental Health First Aid curriculum, “one in five teens has had a serious mental health disorder at some point in their life.” Some effects can last into adulthood and can possibly affect one’s whole life.
