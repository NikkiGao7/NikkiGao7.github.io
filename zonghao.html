Most Common types of Mental Illnesses.

There are over 400 different types of mental illnesses, but the five common types of mental illnesses are anxiety, bipolar disorder, depression, eating Disorders.

Bipolar disorder

Bipolar disorder is a mood disorder that can cause intense mood swings: different types of bipolar disorders rang from

  • - Bipolar I disorder,
  • - Bipolar II disorder,
  • - Cyclothymic disorder.
From Bipolar I disorder being the least sever, and Cyclothymic disorder being the most sever. (requires hospital attention)

Depression

Depression is the make up of different mental conditions. Symptoms include

  • - feeling sad or "empty"
  • - loss of interest in favorite activities, overeating
  • - not wanting to eat at all Not being able to sleep, or sleeping too much,
  • - feeling very tired,
  • - Feeling hopeless, irritable, anxious,
  • - guiltyches or pains, headaches,
  • - cramps,
  • - digestive problems
  • - thoughts of death or suicide

Eating Disorders

There are three major types of eating disorders, These disorders include

  • - Binge-eating, which is out-of-control eating.People with binge-eating disorder tend to keep eating even after they are full.
  • - Bulimia nervosa. People with bulimia nervosa also have periods of binge-eating, but tend to make themselves throw up or using laxatives afterwards.
  • - Anorexia nervosa. People with anorexia nervosa avoid food, severely restrict food, or eat very small quantities of only certain foods

    • Anxiety

    Symptoms of anxiety can be feelings of fear, dread, and uneasiness. Causing you to sweat, feel restless and tense, and have a rapid heartbeat. These tend to happen in stressful situations, where you are too nervous, or stressed.

    PTSD

    PTSD is short for Post-traumatic stress disorder. PTSD is a mental disorder that is developed after a person experiences or see a traumatizing event. These events typically include combat, a natural disaster, a car accident, or sexual assault, but sometimes events do not have to dangerous.
