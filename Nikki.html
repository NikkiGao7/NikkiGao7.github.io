Mental Health/Illness Awareness in the U.S

Mental health impacts everyone in many ways, thus being aware of it is significant. Mental health affects how people cope with stress, their ability to connect with others and make healthy decisions. An individual’s mental health can also impact their physical health as well; those with poor mental health are more likely to have heart attacks, strokes, chronic diseases, sleeping and smoking problems. In addition, poor mental health can lead to mental illnesses. Fortunately, being aware of mental well-being can help. Awareness can assist someone to understand their mental needs, which then allows them to take actions before their issues grows worse.

Mental Illness

With about 1 in 5 Americans experiencing a mental illness each year, mental illnesses are some of the most frequently occurring health conditions in the U.S. However, over 50% of the people struggling with mental disorders do not receive treatment or reach out for assistance. Avoiding help is often a result of the stigma associated with mental illnesses: many people who are diagnosed with mental disorders face prejudice, discrimination, and shame that lead to further suffering because of their disorders. This stigma commonly originates from a lack of accurate knowledge on mental illnesses. According to MentalHelp.net, “...the average person considers himself or herself only slightly informed about mental illness.” If more awareness was brought on these health conditions, the ostracism and unjust treatment faced by the diagnosed could be suppressed, guiding more people to receive help.

Bring awareness to mental health and mental illnesses:

Educate yourself and others:

  • Learn about mental health disorders through people who have experienced what it is like to have mental illnesses. This can be done with individuals you know personally. Many influencers and celebrities have also shared their experiences with mental health challenges via social media.
  • Research mental health and mental illnesses
  • Use social media to share what you have learned
  • Respond to and correct misinformation, harmful perceptions and statements using facts
